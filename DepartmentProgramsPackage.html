Insider Risk Programs in Canada

Named insider risk and insider threat management programs across Canadian government departments and agencies

Department of Justice Canada (DOJ)

Active

Justice operates a formal Insider Risk Program with mandatory learning. Courses include Justice Insider Risk Program for Employees (HWP-001) and Insider Risk Awareness (HWP-002), covering indicators, reporting pathways, and safeguarding responsibilities.

Lead & governance

Safety, Security and Emergency Management Division, Corporate Services Branch (Departmental Security Officer).

Sources

JusCampus – HWP-001 JusCampus – HWP-002

Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC)

2024

Proactive disclosure shows a contract for an independent review of ESDC’s Insider Threat Program—evidence of a mature governance cycle with third-party challenge.

Departmental reporting outlines internal enablers and oversight linkages relevant to safeguarding ESDC-controlled data and investigations.

Lead & governance

Office of the Departmental Security Officer (DSO).

Sources

ESDC contract – Independent Review ESDC – Privacy Act Annual Report 2023–24

Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI)

2024–2025

OSFI competitively awarded a contract for Insider Risk Management Services (supplier: Accenture Inc.) running Feb 26, 2024 to Mar 31, 2025 to support its InRM program. OSFI also issued an Integrity & Security Guideline to FRFIs (Jan 31, 2024) emphasizing governance against foreign interference and related risks.

Organizational materials show insider-risk activities within Corporate Services under the Office of the Chief Security Officer.

Lead & governance

Office of the Chief Security Officer (CSO), Corporate Services; works with Security and Facilities Services (SFS).

Sources

Contract – Insider Risk Mgmt Services OSFI – Integrity & Security Guideline OSFI – ATIP org. context (CSO)

Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)

2023–present

The Insider Risk Management (Guardian) Program is a long-term, continuous-assurance initiative that protects CRA information, assets, systems, and revenues. Phase I (implemented April 1, 2023) uses manual detection models and shares alerts internally for assessment. Indicators are grouped across CRA network activity, HR information, and Security Branch information.

The program applies a People First approach and is aligned to emerging GC continuous-assurance policy. CRA registered a dedicated Personal Information Bank for Guardian (CRA PPU 921).

Lead & governance

Insider Risk Monitoring Section (IRMS), within the Personnel Risk Assessment Division (PRAD), Security Services Directorate, Security Branch.

Sources

Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)

2021–present

Departmental planning commits to implementing “a fulsome and robust insider risk program” including a Security Event Reporting Program. Prior results reports note ongoing enhancement of the Insider Threat Program as a key mitigation activity.

Lead & governance

Departmental Security, RCMP HQ (Corporate Management & Comptrollership).

Sources

RCMP Departmental Plan 2023–24 (PDF) RCMP DRR 2021–22

Global Affairs Canada (GAC)

2022–present

GAC annually reports founded cases and investigative mechanisms. The Personnel Security Services (CSCP) mandate explicitly includes investigations into possible insider threats; Corporate IT Security (CSCI) conducts forensics on network misuse under CSO mandate.

Lead & governance

Corporate Security (incl. Deputy CSO, Personnel Security & Contracting; Corporate IT Security).

Sources

GAC – Misconduct & Wrongdoing 2023–24 GAC report 2022–23 (CSCP/CSCI mandates) GEDS – Corporate Security / DCSO

Transport Canada (TC)

Ongoing

The Transportation Security Clearance (TSC) program mitigates insider risk in aviation and marine by vetting personnel who need unescorted access to restricted areas. In 2025 TC consulted on expanding TSC at ports specifically “to reduce the risk of insider threats.”

Lead & governance

Security and Emergency Management (incl. Aviation Security) – administers TSC and related vetting initiatives.

Sources

Transportation Security Clearance (TSC) 2025 consultation – insider threat at ports

Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA)

Ongoing

CBSA maintains structured prevention, detection and response pathways (professional standards investigations; reporting). Project Edony—originally designed to counter insider threat—has since been re-tasked to synthetic opioid threats in parcel and postal streams, evidencing enterprise coordination across threat areas.

Lead & governance

Security and Professional Standards / Internal Affairs; values and ethics oversight supports misconduct reporting.

Sources

Misconduct investigations & discipline (stats) Fact sheet – Project Edony note Fact sheet – Edony (Dec 12, 2024)

Department of National Defence / Canadian Armed Forces (DND/CAF)

Ongoing

DND/CAF runs an enterprise insider-threat awareness and response program with published guidance and manager aids on behavioural indicators and reporting channels. Counter-intelligence support is provided by the Canadian Forces National Counter-Intelligence Unit (CFNCIU).

Lead & governance

Director General Defence Security (DGDS), under the Vice Chief of the Defence Staff (VCDS).
